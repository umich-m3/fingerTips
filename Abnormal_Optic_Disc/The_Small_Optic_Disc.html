Abnormal Optic Discs

The Small Optic Disc

    • What is it?
    • Optic disc that appears small on ophthalmoscopy
    • Can be monocular or binocular
    • Reflects congenital hypoplasia (reduced axon content)
    • Other forebrain anomalies may be present
    • Maternal diabetes associated with rare binocular variant called “superior segmental optic disc hypoplasia” (“topless optic discs”)

    What does it look like?
    • Core clinical features
      • Optic disc appears to have small diameter on ophthalmoscopy
      • Trap: normal size of optic disc is difficult to assess by ophthalmoscopy, especially in newborns
      • Inner pigment ring may surround margin of optic disc; outer pigment ring may surround peripapillary retinal-choroidal atrophy (“double-ring sign”)
      • Optic discs may appear “topless” with dense inferior nerve fiber bundle visual field defects in patients born to insulin-dependent mothers
    • Imaging features
      • Small-caliber optic nerve
      • Absent septum pellucidum and atrophic optic chiasm (de Morsier syndrome)
      • Other forebrain anomalies

    What else looks like it?
    • Normal optic disc

    What should you do?
    • Look for small optic discs if forebrain or pituitary abnormalities have been found
    • Order brain MRI if ophthalmoscopy suggests small optic disc
    • Tip: forebrain abnormalities more likely if small optic discs present in both eyes
    • Anticipate deficient growth hormone and adrenocorticotropic hormone, which create risk of sudden death in febrile illness, especially if pre-contrast T1 MRI sequence shows “posterior pituitary bright spot”
      to be absent or upwardly displaced
    • Some patients with small optic discs will have reduced vision, but…
    • Trap: avoid making judgment about visual potential in infants, as optic disc size is difficult to estimate at that age and correlation between optic disc size and visual function is poor

    What will happen?
    • Pituitary hormone deficiency is more likely if optic nerve hypoplasia is bilateral and other forebrain anomalies are present
    • Visual function will remain stable
