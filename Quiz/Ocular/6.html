A 45 year old woman reports episodes of blurred vision coincident with a twitching sensation in her left eye.

Review Topic

This disturbance is called

A) Superior oblique myokymia

Correct! You are looking at intorsional oscillations of the left eye, a condition called superior oblique myokymia. It is caused by intermittent activation of the superior oblique muscle, either by instability in the contractile properties of the muscle or by impaired conduction in the fourth nerve. Its cause is usually unknown. Patients report momentary diplopia, blurred vision, and a twitch in the affected eye. Medications that reduce muscle irritability or nerve conduction sometimes work. Brain imaging and other investigations are unnecessary unless there is some other evidence of brainstem or peripheral nerve dysfunction. This condition is often misdiagnosed as nystagmus, but the clues are the unique twitching sensation and the fact that the eye movement is strictly monocular!
