This 45 year old woman reports that her right eye feels shrouded. A plastic surgeon has diagnosed ptosis and has suggested lid-lifting surgery.

    • Review Topic

    What is your diagnosis?

    A) Facial contracture

    Correct! Yes, there is a droopy right upper lid. But notice also that the right brow is slightly lower than the left brow, and that the right lower lid is higher than the left lower lid. This combination of abnormalities suggests tightening of the right facial muscles. There are three reasons for this appearance: 1) recovery from a facial palsy (“post paretic facial contracture”); 2) volitional hemifacial contraction, which cannot be maintained with any persistence; and 3) hemifacial spasm, which typically occurs episodically, not persistently. This patient reported a history of “Bell’s palsy” several years earlier. Her right lower face, not visualized here, was slightly contracted in repose, and had relatively weaker contraction when activated than did the normal left facial muscles. When she activated her orbicularis oculi, her right orbicularis oris also contracted, indicating aberrant synkinesis in a regenerated right facial nerve. Post-paretic facial contracture is common after Bell’s (idiopathic) facial palsy. Lid-lifting surgery is not indicated. Low-dose botulinum toxin injection into the affected orbicularis oculi and cheek muscles may be therapeutic. The alternative answers to this question do not cause facial contracture.
