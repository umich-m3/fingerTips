A 60 year old woman is noted to have pupils that do not constrict fully to light. She did not notice that abnormality and has no symptoms.

Review Topic

What is causing this abnormality?

A) Previous cataract surgery (post-operative “atonic pupils”)

Incorrect

B) Systemic anticholinergic medication

Incorrect

C) Bilateral tonic (Adie) pupils

Incorrect

D) Dorsal midbrain syndrome

Correct!
This video shows pupils viewed in infrared illumination (in darkness). They do not constrict properly to direct light, but they constrict briskly when the patient views a target within 1/3 meter. This is an example of “light-near dissociation.” The brisk constriction to a near target and redilatation when gaze is shifted back to viewing a distant target means that these are not tonic (Adie) pupils. The patient also has bilateral lid retraction and a failure of upward gaze. These are the features of a dorsal midbrain syndrome. The cause in this patient was a pineal tumor.
