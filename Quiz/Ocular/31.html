C) Dissociated vertical deviation

Correct! The eyes have a fine torsional nystagmus, which at first raised concern for a brainstem disorder. But the key abnormality is demonstrated on alternate cover examination. When the cover is removed from the right eye, it descends; when the cover is removed from the left eye, it also descends! In any acquired vertical misalignment of the eyes, and in congenital overaction of the inferior oblique muscles, one eye must move up and the other eye must move down in the cover test. Therefore, this is a distinctive congenital dysinnervation syndrome, probably related to midbrain dysfunction, but unseen in any brain imaging and no threat to the patientâ€™s health.
