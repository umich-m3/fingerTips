C) Multiple sclerosis (MS)

Correct! This is acquired pendular nystagmus. The eyes are oscillating with two slow phases. The waveform is complex. The eyes have a circular or elliptical trajectory—“multivector nystagmus” or, casually, “egg-beater nystagmus.” MS affecting the brainstem is the most common cause, but brainstem tumors, trauma, and metabolic disorders can also be responsible. Acute disseminated encephalomyelitis could also cause this, but less commonly than MS. Pineal tumors do not typically cause nystagmus, but if they do, it will be upbeat. Dorsolateral medullary (Wallenberg) infarct certainly causes nystagmus, but its waveform is typically horizontal-rotary jerk.
