A 27 year old man undergoing a routine optometric examination is found to have this test result on testing of pupils:

Review Topic

The responsible lesion could be in any of these locations EXCEPT

A) Optic radiations

Correct! You have been told that this test is showing a right (relative) afferent pupil defect. The most likely location of the lesion is the RIGHT optic nerve, but there could be lesions in BOTH optic nerves, so long as the lesion in the RIGHT optic nerve is more damaging than the lesion in the LEFT optic nerve (this test compares afferent visual input in the two optic nerves). The lesion could also lie in the left optic tract because the retinal ganglion cell axons that cross in the optic chiasm outnumber those that do not cross. The lesion could not be in the optic radiations, which lie posterior to the pupillary reflex arc and hence do not affect pupil constriction to light.

B) Right optic nerve

Incorrect

C) Both optic nerves

Incorrect

D) Optic tract

Incorrect
