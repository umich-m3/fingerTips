64. The patient in this video is viewing an optokinetic strip moved first to his left and then to his right.

Review Topic

What abnormality is this maneuver bringing out?

A) Internuclear ophthalmoplegia

Incorrect

B) Sixth nerve palsy

Correct! As the optokinetic strip is moved to the patient’s left, it evokes involuntary leftward conjugate pursuit, followed by rightward conjugate recovery saccades in a repetitive sequence that allows you to see that both eyes are making decent rightward saccades. But when the strip is moved to the patient’s right, the evoked saccades in the left eye have much lower amplitude than the evoked saccades in the right eye, bringing out an abducting saccadic deficit in the left eye--an eye that has a sixth nerve palsy. The optokinetic strip (or drum) maneuver has five main clinical uses: 1) displaying a subtle abduction deficit in sixth nerve palsy, as you saw here; 2) displaying a subtle internuclear ophthalmoplegia, which would be manifest by relatively low-amplitude ADDUCTING saccades; 3) displaying convergence retraction of the eyes on attempted upgaze in dorsal midbrain syndrome; 4) displaying a relatively reduced contraversive saccadic amplitude IN BOTH EYES when moved in the direction of a damaged parietal lobe; 5) displaying intact nystagmus in a patient with factitiously impaired sight.

C) Dorsal midbrain syndrome

Incorrect

D) Right parietal lobe dysfunction

Incorrect
