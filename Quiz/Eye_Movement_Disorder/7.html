This 68 year old woman reported difficulty seeing the stairs on descent and in eating her food. Observe the abnormalities in her eye movements.

Review Topic

What assessment provided the critical localizing information?

A) Pursuit

Incorrect

B) Saccades

Incorrect

C) Vestibulo-ocular reflex

Correct! This patient has impaired volitional gaze--saccades and pursuit--in both the horizontal and vertical planes. Volitional gaze in the vertical plane is virtually absent. Yet passive movement of her head—the doll’s eye or oculocephalic maneuver--elicits vertical eye movement. These features tell us that the gaze disturbance spares the pathway from the vestibular end organs through to their connections to the ocular motor nuclei. The lesion must therefore lie above these ocular motor nuclei in the hierarchy of ocular motor control. Such a disturbance is termed “supranuclear.” Other clinical features—reduced spontaneous facial movements and gestures, axial rigidity, and frequent unexplained falls—were also present in this patient, defining a condition known as “progressive supranuclear palsy,” a degenerative disorder for which there is sadly no effective treatment. Precautions against falling and aspiration must be taken.
