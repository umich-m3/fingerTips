A 50 year old woman reports that vision has gradually become indistinct in both eyes. Visual acuity is 20/60 (6/18, 0.3) in each eye.

    • Review Topic

    What SIMPLE maneuver would establish an optical cause for the subnormal vision?

    A) Pinhole examination

    Correct! If you place a single or multiple pinhole occlude in front of each eye and discover that visual acuity markedly improves, you have assigned the problem to the optic segment. It may be an uncorrected refractive error of a defect in the cornea or lens that interferes with a point focus on the fovea. If visual acuity does not substantially improve with the pinhole test, then the problem lies in the retinocortical segment or the patient is unable or unwilling to cooperate with the test, providing a “false negative” result. If you do not have a pinhole occluder, make one by puncturing one or more millimeter holes in a piece of paper (more holes is better). Proper pupil, visual field, and ophthalmoscopic examinations provide a lot of information, but are not as well suited to solve this issue
