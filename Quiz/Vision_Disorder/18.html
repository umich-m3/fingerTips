    C) Toxic optic neuropathy

    Correct! The bilateral centrocecal scotomas on the visual field examination are the chief clues to localization and pathogenesis! Such defects occur from selective damage to the unmyelinated optic nerve axons emanating from the fovea and retina lying between the fovea and optic disc. Toxic, nutritional, and hereditary optic neuropathies do that kind of damage. Optic neuritis typically preferentially affects myelinated axons and would not be expected to produce such symmetrical effects. Compressive and inflammatory conditions also produce asymmetric damage to the optic nerves. This patient had been treated with ethambutol for tuberculosis. Optic nerve toxicity is rare at daily ethambutol doses below 15mg/kg unless the patient has reduced clearance from kidney disease.
