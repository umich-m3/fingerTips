What is it?
  • Assessment of peripheral (non-foveal) achromatic vision
  • Complements testing of visual acuity, which assesses central (foveal) achromatic vision (Visual Aucity)

What should you do?
  • Perform Amsler Grid test to screen for small defects near fixation
  • Trap: Amsler Grid test requires patients to outline their own visual field defects; they must see them distinctly to do so
  • Trap: Amsler Grid test is useful only for detects within 10 degrees of fixation
  • Perform confrontation visual field testing to screen for large defects
  • Trap: confrontation visual field testing detects only dense hemianopias and altitudinal defects
  • Perform bowl perimetry, the most sensitive method to detect visual field defects
  • Trap: although bowl perimetry is sensitive to defects, it requires patient attention and cooperation--often missing!
