Nystagmus-like Oscillations

Square Wave Jerks

    • What is it?
    • Horizontal oscillating back-to-back saccades that interrupt fixation
    • Normal variant is of low amplitude and infrequent, but sign of brainstem dysfunction if prominent
    • No symptoms
    • Common causes: progressive supranuclear palsy (PSP), cerebellar degenerations, brainstem trauma, multiple sclerosis
    • Uncommon causes: Parkinson disease, Huntington disease, schizophrenia

    What does it look like?
    • Horizontal nonrhythmic saccades that carry both eyes in one direction away from fixation and then, after a pause (“intersaccadic interval”), bring them back to fixation Square Wave Jerks
    • May be normal if amplitude <5º and infrequent Square Wave Jerks Normal Subject
    • May be accompanied by saccadic pursuit, hypometric saccades, and other neurologic deficits

    What else looks like it?
    • Saccades of inattention or thinking Fixation Instability of Inattention
    • Infantile nystagmus syndrome Infantile Nystagmus Syndrome
    • Acquired pendular nystagmus Acquired Pendular Nystagmus
    • Ocular dysmetria Ocular Dysmetria
    • Ocular flutter Ocular Flutter
    • Volitional flutter Volitional Flutter

    What should you do?
    • Dismiss as normal variant if the movements are of low amplitude, infrequent, and isolated
    • Pay attention to the imitators, especially saccades of inattention or thinking, ocular flutter, volitional flutter, and ocular dysmetria

    What will happen?
    • Square wave jerks persist
    • Tip: they usually do not impair vision, except if frequent enough to reduce foveation time
    • Trap: they are often mistaken for nystagmus, causing fear and fruitless evaluation
