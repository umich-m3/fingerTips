Chiasmal Segment

    • Video Overview

    What is it?
    • Visual pathway segment that conveys neural signals through optic chiasm

    What does it do?
    • Redirects path of optic nerve axons so that they will eventually represent vision in cerebral hemispheres according to hemifields rather than as single eye fields Chiasmal Sorting Revised
    • Completes this task by having axons from nasal retina cross to contralateral optic tract and having axons from temporal retina enter ipsilateral optic tract

    How does it go wrong?
    • Unilateral temporal hemianopic defect: lesion lies in ipsilateral optic nerve, damaging primarily fibers from ipsilateral nasal retina which are especially vulnerable
    • Temporal hemianopic defect in one eye and nerve fiber bundle defect in other eye: lesion lies at junction of optic chiasm, damaging ipsilateral optic nerve and axons from contralateral nasal retina that loop into ipsilateral optic nerve
    • Bitemporal hemianopic defects: lesion lies in or around optic chiasm, preferentially damaging nasal crossing fibers, which are especially vulnerable
