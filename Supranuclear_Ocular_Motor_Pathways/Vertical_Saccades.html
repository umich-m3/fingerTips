Supranuclear Ocular Motor Pathways

Vertical Saccades

    • Video Overview
      Video

    What is it?
    • Neural pathway that initiates voluntary upward and downward saccades

    How does it work?
    • Signal goes to visual cortex,
      then to occipital-parietal and frontal gaze centers in both hemispheres;
      from both those cerebral hemisphere gaze centers,
      the signal goes down to the superior colliculus
      and on to the rostral interstitial nucleus of the MLF (riMLF)
      to initiate vertical saccades; the signal also goes to the interstitial nucleus of Cajal (INC)
      to maintain eccentric gaze
    • Upward saccades
      • Signal goes from riMLF dorsally through the posterior commissure, ventrally to the superior rectus and inferior oblique subnuclei of the third cranial nerve, and from there to muscles innervated by those nuclei
    • Downward saccades
      • Signal goes through tegmental midbrain to superior oblique nucleus and inferior rectus subnucleus and then to the muscles innervated by those nuclei

    How does it go wrong?
    • Bilateral cerebral hemisphere lesions
      • Impairs vertical saccades and pursuit
      • Spares vertical vestibulo-ocular reflex because vestibulo-ocular pathway is restricted to brainstem
      • Tip: impairment of saccades and pursuit with sparing of vestibulo-ocular reflex is called “supranuclear gaze palsy” PSP revised
    • Dorsal (tectal) midbrain lesion
      • Dorsal (tectal) midbrain lesion
      • Other features
        • Upbeat nystagmus
        • Downgaze deviation
        • Skew deviation
        • Convergence retraction on attempted upgaze Convergence Retraction
        • Tectal pupils (large pupils with poor constriction to light but preserved constriction to near target)
        • Fourth nerve palsy
        • Esotropia
        • Exotropia
      • Ventral (tegmental) midbrain lesion
        • Impaired downgaze saccades, pursuit, and vestibulo-ocular reflex Downgaze Paresis
        • Other features
          • Downbeat nystagmus
          • Upgaze deviation
          • Skew deviation
          • Loss of pupil construction to near target
          • Esotropia
          • Exotropia
          • Ataxia
